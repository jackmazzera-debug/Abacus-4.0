ⓘ Trim to Adjusted Target — How it works

When positions are manually excluded (e.g. restricted securities), their model weights are redistributed proportionally across the remaining positions. This creates a new adjusted target for each included position that is slightly higher than the original.

In this mode, overweight positions are only sold down to their adjusted target — not their original target. This means:

  • A position that is overweight vs its original target may actually be at or below its adjusted target and will not be sold
  • A position that is still overweight vs its adjusted target will be trimmed to that higher threshold
  • The result is a portfolio aligned to the rescaled model weights, accounting for the excluded positions

Use this mode when you have restricted or legacy positions that cannot be traded and you want the rest of the portfolio to rebalance around them naturally.

Getting started

Upload your Tamarac rebalance export (.txt file) using the upload area at the top of the page, or paste the raw text directly. The tool will parse every position in the file and load them into the calculator. Once loaded, set your cash to deploy and configure the options below.

Calculator controls

Cash to Deploy

The dollar amount of new cash you are adding to the portfolio. This is invested on top of existing positions — it does not represent the total portfolio value. Defaults to $0. Enter your wire amount or available cash balance here.

Exclude overweights from buys

When checked, any position that is already above its model target is excluded from receiving new cash. Use this when you are adding cash only and do not want to touch existing overweight positions — for example, when trading around embedded gains in a taxable account or managing a restricted position. Cash is deployed pro-rata across underweight positions based on the size of their gap to target.

When unchecked, the sell mode bar appears and the tool enters full rebalance mode — overweight positions can be trimmed and proceeds redeployed alongside your new cash.

Only add to currently held positions

When checked, positions that are in the model but currently have a $0 balance are excluded from receiving new cash. Useful when you want to build existing positions before initiating new ones.

Whole shares only

When checked, all buy and sell quantities are rounded down to the nearest whole share. Any undeployed cash from rounding appears as a remainder in the summary. Recommended for most use cases — leave unchecked only if your custodian supports fractional shares.

Sell mode

Sell mode is only active when Exclude overweights from buys is unchecked. It controls how aggressively overweight positions are trimmed before proceeds are redeployed.

Trim to Original Target

Sells each overweight position down to its exact model target dollar value as imported from Tamarac. This is the most aggressive trim — it brings all overweights fully back to model before redeploying. Use when you want a clean rebalance with no overweights remaining.

Trim to Adjusted Target

Sells overweights down to their adjusted target — the rescaled model weight after accounting for any manually hidden/restricted positions. When you exclude a position using the row checkbox, its model weight is redistributed proportionally to all remaining positions, raising their ideal targets slightly. In this mode, a position that appears overweight vs its original target may actually be at or below its adjusted target and will not be sold. Use this when you have restricted or legacy positions that can't be traded and want the rest of the portfolio to rebalance naturally around them.

Trim to 125% Threshold (default)

Only sells the portion of a position that sits above 125% of its model target. This is the most conservative trim — positions that are overweight but within 125% of target are left untouched. Use when you want to trim only the most extreme outliers without triggering unnecessary trades.

Hiding / restricting positions

Use the Excl. checkbox on any row to manually hide a position from the calculator. Hidden positions are shown in gray with a left border indicator so they remain visible but clearly marked as inactive.

When a position is hidden, its model weight is automatically redistributed proportionally across all remaining included positions. The Model Target column will show both the original target and the adjusted target (labeled "adj" in blue) for any position whose target has been rescaled.

Common use cases: restricted securities, positions with wash sale considerations, legacy holdings that cannot be traded, or positions you want to intentionally skip in a given rebalance.

Pre / Post Report tab

This tab shows the impact of the proposed trades on portfolio alignment. It includes:

  • Portfolio value — total current value vs projected post-trade value
  • Model deviation (RMSD) — a measure of how far the portfolio sits from its model weights before and after the trade. Lower is better. A falling RMSD means the trades are moving the portfolio closer to model.
  • Out of tolerance count — number of positions outside the 75%–125% tolerance band pre and post trade
  • Top 5 overweight / underweight — the five largest deviations from model target, shown both before and after the proposed trades

Use this tab to sanity-check a rebalance before executing. If the post-trade RMSD is not lower than the pre-trade RMSD, the trade set is not improving model alignment.

Tax Dashboard tab

The tax dashboard surfaces unrealized gain and loss data parsed directly from the Tamarac export. It includes:

  • Total unrealized ST / LT — portfolio-wide short-term and long-term gain/loss totals
  • Tax loss harvest candidates — positions that meet both the dollar loss and percentage loss thresholds you set. Adjust the thresholds using the input fields at the top of the section.
  • Largest unrealized losses / gains — ranked lists of the top 8 positions by unrealized gain or loss

TLH candidates are identified by two criteria that must both be met: the total unrealized loss must exceed the dollar threshold and the loss as a percentage of cost basis must exceed the % threshold. Both fields are adjustable. Default is losses greater than $5,000 and greater than 5%.

Exports

  • PDF Report — generates a formatted landscape PDF including the portfolio summary, top overweight/underweight positions, sell orders (if applicable), buy orders, and TLH candidates. Suitable for printing or sharing with the PM team.
  • Excel Trade Blotter — generates a multi-tab .xlsx workbook. The Sells tab lists all sell orders, the Buys tab lists all buy orders, the Full Snapshot tab shows every position with pre and post values, and the TLH Candidates tab lists positions meeting your harvest threshold.
